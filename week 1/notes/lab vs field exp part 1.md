## Controlled Environments: Induced-Value Theory (Smith 2002)

	Why do we need Induced-Value Theory? 
	Why is it important to have subjects perform like real-world subjects?
	Proper use of a reward medium allows an experimenter to induce pre-specified characteristics in experimental subjects
	Patient is rewarded based on their performance.

- (3) conditions	
	- Monotonicity: prefer more reward medium to less
	- Salience: reward depends on subject actions
	- Dominance: change in subject utility come predominantly from the reward medium
		- importance of privacy

## Induced-Value Theory: Practice Implications in Design

- Pay subjects cash
	- Grade?
